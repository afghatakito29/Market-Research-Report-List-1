<p><p>The food grade microcrystalline cellulose market is primarily divided into two types: wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based. Wood pulp-based microcrystalline cellulose is derived from natural wood fibers, offering a cost-effective and widely available option, commonly used as a bulking agent and stabilizer in food products. Refined cotton-based microcrystalline cellulose, produced from cotton linters, is valued for its purity and superior properties, making it suitable for applications requiring high-quality functional ingredients. Both types serve diverse roles in food formulation and manufacturing.</p></p>
