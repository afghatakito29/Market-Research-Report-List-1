<p><p>The farm irrigation systems market encompasses several types, each with distinct features. Flood irrigation involves inundating fields with water, suitable for flat terrains. Sprinkler irrigation mimics rainfall by distributing water through pipes and nozzles, ideal for various soil types. Drip irrigation delivers water directly to plant roots through a network of tubes, promoting efficiency and reducing waste. Micro irrigation, similar to drip, applies small amounts of water to specific areas, supporting precise watering needs. Each system caters to different agricultural demands and environmental conditions.</p></p>
